SRM-Met | Weather and Climate Extremes Research Group

Collaborative Research

SRM-Met actively promotes interdisciplinary and collaborative research in atmospheric sciences, weather and climate extremes, lightning science, atmospheric electricity, numerical weather prediction, satellite meteorology and artificial intelligence applications.

The research group welcomes collaborations with universities, research laboratories, government agencies, industries and international research organizations to address scientific and societal challenges related to weather, climate and environmental sustainability.

Strategic Research Partnerships and MoUs

SRM-Met has established formal collaborative arrangements through Memoranda of Understanding (MoUs) to strengthen observational capabilities, data sharing, and advanced atmospheric science research.

ISRO – ISTRAC Collaboration

SRM-Met collaborates with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) through the ISRO Telemetry, Tracking and Command Network (ISTRAC). Under this collaboration, a LASER Disdrometer supports research on rainfall microphysics, raindrop size distributions, precipitation processes, and validation of satellite-derived rainfall products.

WWLLN – University of Washington Collaboration

SRM-Met hosts a Lightning Sensor under a collaborative arrangement with the Worldwide Lightning Location Network (WWLLN), University of Washington, USA. The facility supports studies on lightning climatology, atmospheric electricity, thunderstorm dynamics, severe weather monitoring and lightning hazard assessment.
