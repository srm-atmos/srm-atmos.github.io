Research Overview

The Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory (ASRL) conducts research on atmospheric processes ranging from thunderstorms and lightning to climate variability and weather extremes. The laboratory combines atmospheric observations, satellite remote sensing, numerical weather prediction, climate modelling and artificial intelligence to improve understanding and prediction of high-impact weather and climate phenomena.

Major Research Areas

Weather and Climate Extremes

Investigation of extreme rainfall, floods, droughts and high-impact weather events over India using observations, climate simulations and numerical modelling.

Lightning Science

Studies of lightning climatology, lightning hazard prediction, atmospheric electricity and thunderstorm electrification processes using observational and modelling approaches.

Thunderstorms and Severe Weather

Research on convective systems, atmospheric instability, boundary layer processes and severe weather mechanisms.

Satellite Meteorology

Applications of INSAT, GPM, MODIS and other satellite observations for weather monitoring, rainfall estimation and climate studies.

Numerical Weather Prediction

High-resolution simulations using the Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) model for process understanding, forecast improvement and sensitivity studies.

Climate Modelling

Evaluation of CMIP6 climate models, climate variability, monsoon dynamics and future climate projections.

Artificial Intelligence

Application of machine learning and physics-informed AI for weather prediction, bias correction and climate analysis.

Atmospheric Electricity

Studies of electric field variability, lightning occurrence, aerosol interactions and atmospheric electrification.

Current Research Projects
