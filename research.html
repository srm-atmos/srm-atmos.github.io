Research Activities

SRM-Met | Weather and Climate Extremes Research Group

Research Overview

SRM-Met conducts interdisciplinary research in atmospheric sciences by integrating atmospheric observations, satellite remote sensing, numerical weather prediction, climate modelling and artificial intelligence. The research group focuses on understanding physical processes governing weather and climate extremes and developing improved forecasting systems for societal applications.

The laboratory combines observations, numerical modelling, satellite meteorology and data-driven approaches to investigate severe weather events, rainfall extremes, thunderstorms, lightning activity and climate variability over India.

Major Research Themes

Weather and Climate Extremes

Investigation of heavy rainfall, floods, droughts, heat waves and other extreme weather events over India with special emphasis on Southern Peninsular India.

Lightning Science

Studies on lightning climatology, lightning hazard prediction, thunderstorm electrification and lightning-producing weather systems.

Atmospheric Electricity

Investigation of atmospheric electric field variability, cloud electrification processes, lightning generation mechanisms and fair-weather electricity.

Numerical Weather Prediction

High-resolution simulations using the Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF-ARW) model to investigate mesoscale weather systems, thunderstorms and extreme rainfall events.

Satellite Meteorology

Applications of INSAT, GPM, TRMM, MODIS and other Earth observation systems for atmospheric monitoring and weather analysis.

Climate Modelling

Assessment of climate variability, climate change and future climate projections using CMIP6 and regional climate modelling frameworks.

Artificial Intelligence

Development of machine learning and physics-informed artificial intelligence approaches for weather forecasting and climate applications.

Atmospheric Boundary Layer Studies

Understanding land-atmosphere interactions, turbulence and boundary layer processes influencing weather and climate.

Numerical Modelling Research

WRF Modelling

High-resolution simulations of thunderstorms, heavy rainfall events and severe weather systems using the WRF-ARW modelling framework.

Data Assimilation

Integration of observations and numerical models for improved weather prediction and atmospheric diagnostics.

Climate Simulations

Regional climate assessments and evaluation of climate model projections using global and regional climate models.

Observational Research

Lightning Observations

WWLLN, ground-based lightning observations and satellite-derived lightning products for severe weather studies.

Atmospheric Monitoring

Measurements using Aethalometer, Sun Photometer, Automatic Weather Station, High Volume Samplers and LIDAR systems.

Satellite Observations

Use of INSAT, GPM, TRMM and other remote sensing datasets for atmospheric process studies and rainfall validation.

High Performance Computing

The research group utilizes the SRM Institute of Science and Technology High Performance Computing Centre (HPCC) for numerical weather prediction, climate modelling, machine learning applications and large-volume atmospheric data analysis.

The HPCC facility supports WRF simulations, climate model evaluation, satellite data processing, artificial intelligence workflows and computational atmospheric science research.
