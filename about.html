About SRM-ATMOS

SRM-ATMOS | Weather and Climate Extremes Research Group

Who We Are

SRM-ATMOS is the Weather and Climate Extremes Research Group of the Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory (ASRL), functioning under the Centre for Atmospheric Sciences and Climate Studies, Department of Physics and Nanotechnology, College of Engineering and Technology, SRM Institute of Science and Technology.

The group conducts interdisciplinary research in atmospheric sciences by integrating observations, satellite remote sensing, numerical weather prediction, climate modelling and artificial intelligence. The laboratory focuses on understanding atmospheric processes associated with high-impact weather and climate events over India and surrounding regions.

Vision

To become a leading research centre in atmospheric sciences through excellence in weather and climate research, innovation in numerical modelling and artificial intelligence, and development of operational solutions for societal applications.

Mission

  • Advance scientific understanding of weather and climate extremes.
  • Develop high-resolution numerical weather prediction capabilities.
  • Investigate lightning processes and atmospheric electricity.
  • Promote satellite meteorology and Earth observation applications.
  • Develop physics-informed artificial intelligence frameworks for atmospheric sciences.
  • Train future scientists through high-quality research and education.
  • Support national and international collaborations in atmospheric research.

Major Research Areas

Weather and Climate Extremes

Investigation of heavy rainfall, floods, droughts and extreme weather events.

Lightning Science

Lightning climatology, hazard prediction and atmospheric electricity studies.

Numerical Weather Prediction

High-resolution simulations using the WRF modelling system.

Satellite Meteorology

Applications of INSAT, GPM, MODIS and other Earth observation systems.

Climate Modelling

Climate variability, climate change and CMIP6 model evaluation.

Artificial Intelligence

Physics-informed AI and machine learning applications in atmospheric sciences.

Research Infrastructure

The laboratory is supported by advanced observational facilities including an WWLLN's lightning sensor, Sun Photometer, ISTRAC's LASER Disdrometer, aerosol monitoring systems and access to High Performance Computing facilities for large-scale numerical simulations.

Collaborations and Outreach

SRM-ATMOS actively promotes interdisciplinary collaborations, student research training, scientific outreach programmes and community engagement activities related to weather, climate and environmental sciences.

The group regularly participates in conferences, workshops, collaborative research initiatives and awareness programmes on climate change and atmospheric hazards.
