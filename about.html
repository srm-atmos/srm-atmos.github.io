About the Laboratory

The Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory (ASRL) is a research laboratory within the Department of Physics and Nanotechnology at SRM Institute of Science and Technology. The laboratory serves as a multidisciplinary platform for research, education, and innovation in atmospheric sciences, with particular emphasis on understanding and predicting high-impact weather and climate events.

ASRL integrates observations, numerical modelling, satellite remote sensing, lightning measurements, climate diagnostics, and artificial intelligence techniques to investigate atmospheric processes across multiple spatial and temporal scales. The laboratory promotes collaborative research aimed at advancing scientific knowledge and supporting societal resilience to weather and climate hazards.

Vision

To establish ASRL as a leading centre of excellence in atmospheric sciences, contributing to scientific advancements in weather prediction, climate variability, severe weather processes, and emerging data-driven technologies for atmospheric research.

Mission

  • Advance scientific understanding of weather and climate extremes.
  • Develop and apply numerical weather prediction systems.
  • Investigate thunderstorm and lightning processes.
  • Utilize satellite observations for atmospheric monitoring.
  • Integrate artificial intelligence with physical modelling approaches.
  • Train future researchers in atmospheric sciences.
  • Promote interdisciplinary and collaborative research.

Research Focus Areas

  • Weather and Climate Extremes
  • Extreme Rainfall and Floods
  • Thunderstorms and Severe Weather
  • Lightning Science and Atmospheric Electricity
  • Satellite Meteorology
  • Numerical Weather Prediction
  • Climate Variability and Change
  • Artificial Intelligence for Atmospheric Sciences
  • Remote Sensing Applications

Facilities and Expertise

The laboratory utilizes observational datasets, satellite products, reanalysis data, lightning observations, and advanced numerical modelling frameworks. Research activities include high-resolution weather simulations, climate diagnostics, satellite-based analyses, machine learning applications, and interdisciplinary studies related to atmospheric hazards.

ASRL actively supports research involving the Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) model, satellite meteorology, lightning climatology, climate diagnostics, and emerging physics-informed artificial intelligence approaches.

Future Directions

The laboratory aims to strengthen research capabilities in weather and climate extremes, atmospheric electrification, satellite remote sensing, numerical weather prediction, and artificial intelligence. Future activities will focus on advancing predictive capabilities, developing innovative research tools, expanding collaborative networks, and training the next generation of atmospheric scientists.
