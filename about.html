About the Laboratory

The Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory (ASRL) is a research laboratory within the Department of Physics and Nanotechnology at SRM Institute of Science and Technology. The laboratory conducts research on weather and climate extremes through the integration of atmospheric observations, lightning measurements, satellite remote sensing, numerical weather prediction, climate modelling and artificial intelligence.

ASRL serves as a multidisciplinary platform for atmospheric research, education and innovation. The laboratory focuses on improving scientific understanding and prediction of high-impact weather and climate events, including extreme rainfall, thunderstorms, lightning, climate variability and atmospheric hazards.

Vision

To establish ASRL as a nationally and internationally recognized centre of excellence in atmospheric sciences through innovative research, advanced modelling, atmospheric observations and artificial intelligence applications for weather and climate prediction.

Mission

  • Advance understanding of weather and climate extremes.
  • Develop innovative approaches for weather prediction.
  • Investigate lightning and atmospheric electricity processes.
  • Apply satellite meteorology and remote sensing techniques.
  • Integrate artificial intelligence with physical modelling.
  • Train future atmospheric scientists.
  • Promote interdisciplinary and collaborative research.

  • Satellite Meteorology and Remote Sensing

Education and Training

The laboratory contributes to postgraduate education and research training in Atmospheric Science through advanced coursework, laboratory instruction, research supervision and hands-on experience in atmospheric data analysis, modelling and observations.

Major training areas include Atmospheric Dynamics, Numerical Weather Prediction, Climate Modelling, Satellite Meteorology, Atmospheric Data Analysis and Artificial Intelligence applications.

Outreach and Societal Impact

ASRL actively participates in climate awareness activities, weather communication, student engagement programmes and scientific outreach initiatives. The laboratory promotes public understanding of weather and climate hazards and supports climate resilience through scientific knowledge dissemination.
