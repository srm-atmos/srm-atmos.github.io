Welcome to SRM-ASRL

The Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory (ASRL) is a research laboratory in the Department of Physics and Nanotechnology at SRM Institute of Science and Technology. The laboratory conducts research on weather and climate extremes, numerical weather prediction, lightning and thunderstorm processes, satellite meteorology, climate variability, and artificial intelligence applications in atmospheric sciences.

Through observations, numerical modeling, remote sensing, and data-driven approaches, the laboratory aims to advance scientific understanding and prediction of high-impact atmospheric phenomena.

Research Themes

Extreme Rainfall & Floods

Heavy rainfall events, floods and monsoon extremes.

Thunderstorms & Severe Weather

Convective systems and severe weather processes.

Lightning Science

Lightning climatology and thunderstorm electrification.

Satellite Meteorology

INSAT, GPM and remote sensing applications.

Numerical Weather Prediction

WRF modelling and forecast verification.

Artificial Intelligence

Physics-informed AI and machine learning applications.
