Welcome to SRM-ASRL

The Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory (ASRL) is a research laboratory in the Department of Physics and Nanotechnology at SRM Institute of Science and Technology. The laboratory conducts research on weather and climate extremes, numerical weather prediction, lightning and thunderstorm processes, satellite meteorology, climate variability, and artificial intelligence applications in atmospheric sciences. The laboratory aims to advance scientific understanding and prediction of high-impact weather and climate events through observations, modeling, and data-driven approaches.

Research Themes

Extreme Rainfall

Heavy rainfall events, floods and monsoon extremes.

Thunderstorms

Convective systems and severe weather processes.

Lightning

Lightning climatology and thunderstorm electrification.

Satellite Meteorology

INSAT, GPM and remote sensing applications.

Numerical Weather Prediction

WRF modelling and forecast verification.

AI for Atmospheric Sciences

Physics-informed AI and machine learning applications.
