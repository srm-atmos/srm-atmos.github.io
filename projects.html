Research Projects and Programmes

SRM-Met | Weather and Climate Extremes Research Group

Project Portfolio

SRM-Met undertakes research projects in weather and climate extremes, atmospheric electricity, lightning science, satellite meteorology, numerical weather prediction, climate modelling and artificial intelligence. The projects integrate atmospheric observations, remote sensing, modelling and data-driven techniques to address scientific and societal challenges.

Flagship Research Projects

Lightning Hazard Prediction over India

This flagship project aims to develop an integrated lightning hazard prediction framework for India by combining advanced numerical weather prediction models, lightning observations and atmospheric diagnostics. The study utilizes the Weather Research and Forecasting model with Electrification (WRF-ELEC) and the National Centre for Medium Range Weather Forecasting Unified Model – Regional (NCUM-R) to investigate atmospheric processes responsible for lightning generation and severe thunderstorm activity.

WRF-ELEC simulations are employed to explicitly represent cloud electrification processes and lightning-producing mechanisms within convective storms. The model is used to examine charge structures, graupel–ice interactions, mixed-phase microphysics, vertical velocity and simulated lightning flash activity during severe weather events.

NCUM-R model outputs are utilized to identify atmospheric conditions favorable for lightning development, including convective available potential energy (CAPE), moisture transport, vertical wind shear, instability indices and precipitation forecasts. These products provide operational forecasting guidance for lightning-prone environments across India.

Model simulations are evaluated using observations from the Worldwide Lightning Location Network (WWLLN), satellite products, reanalysis datasets and surface observations. The project aims to contribute towards lightning early-warning systems, disaster risk reduction and public safety.

Integrated WRF and Physics-Informed AI Framework

Development of a hybrid framework combining numerical weather prediction, physical process understanding and Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) for improved prediction and interpretation of rainfall extremes over Southern Peninsular India.

Extreme Rainfall over Southern Peninsular India

Investigation of heavy rainfall events, flood-producing systems, monsoon dynamics and mesoscale atmospheric processes using observations, reanalysis products and high-resolution WRF simulations.

Atmospheric Electricity and Lightning Research

Thunderstorm Electrification

Investigation of electrical charging mechanisms and lightning production within convective storms.

Lightning Climatology

Analysis of spatial and temporal lightning distributions using WWLLN and regional lightning datasets.

Atmospheric Electric Field Studies

Research on atmospheric electricity, fair-weather electric fields and thunderstorm electrical environments.

Weather Forecasting and Modelling Projects

High-Resolution WRF Simulations

Operational Forecast Development

Development of automated WRF-based weather forecasting systems for research and operational applications.

Data Assimilation Studies

Integration of observations and model simulations to improve weather prediction capabilities.
