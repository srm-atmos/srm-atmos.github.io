Facilities

SRM-Met | Weather and Climate Extremes Research Group

Research Infrastructure

SRM-Met maintains observational and computational facilities to support research in atmospheric sciences, weather and climate extremes, aerosol science, lightning studies, rain microphysics and numerical weather prediction.

The laboratory integrates atmospheric observations, advanced instrumentation and high-performance computing resources to investigate weather and climate processes over India and surrounding regions.

Atmospheric Aerosol Monitoring Facilities

Aethalometer

Measurement of black carbon aerosol concentrations and aerosol absorption characteristics for air quality and climate studies.

Sun Photometer

Observation of aerosol optical depth and atmospheric column properties for aerosol-climate interaction studies.

High Volume Sampler

Collection of particulate matter samples for aerosol characterization and environmental monitoring.

PM₁ Sampler

Monitoring of fine particulate matter (PM₁) for air quality and health-related studies.

PM₂.₅ Sampler

Measurement of PM₂.₅ concentrations for urban air quality and atmospheric pollution assessments.

Precipitation Measurement Facility

Laser Disdrometer

Laser Disdrometer observations are available under a collaborative arrangement through a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with ISTRAC, ISRO.

The instrument provides detailed information on raindrop size distribution, rainfall intensity and precipitation microphysics, supporting studies on extreme rainfall events, cloud microphysics and numerical model validation.

Lightning Observation Facility

Lightning Sensor

A lightning monitoring sensor is operated under a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the Worldwide Lightning Location Network (WWLLN), University of Washington, USA.

The system supports studies on lightning climatology, thunderstorm electrification, severe weather monitoring and lightning hazard assessment.

Computational Facilities

High Performance Computing Centre (HPCC)

Researchers at SRM-Met have access to the High Performance Computing Centre (HPCC) of SRM Institute of Science and Technology.

The computational resources are utilized for:

  • Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) simulations
  • Regional climate modelling
  • Climate data analysis
  • Satellite data processing
  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning applications
  • Large-volume atmospheric datasets
  • Forecast verification and diagnostics
