Facilities and Research Infrastructure

Atmospheric Science Research Laboratory

Weather and Climate Extremes Research Group

Overview

SRM-ATMOS maintains advanced observational, computational and atmospheric monitoring facilities supporting research in weather and climate extremes, atmospheric electricity, lightning science, aerosol studies, satellite meteorology and numerical weather prediction.

Facility Highlights

Mini Micro Pulse LIDAR

Advanced remote sensing instrument used for aerosol profiling, boundary-layer studies and atmospheric monitoring.

Aethalometer

Measurement of black carbon aerosols and atmospheric particulate matter for air quality and climate studies.

Automatic Weather Station

Continuous observations of meteorological parameters supporting weather and climate research.

Laser Precipitation Monitor

High-resolution precipitation measurements for rainfall microphysics and hydrometeorological studies.

Computational Facilities

High Performance Computing (HPCC)

SRM-ATMOS researchers have access to the High Performance Computing Centre (HPCC) at SRM Institute of Science and Technology for large-scale numerical simulations and data processing.

Visit SRMIST HPCC

WRF Numerical Modelling

High-resolution Weather Research and Forecasting (WRF) modelling system for weather prediction and process studies.

Climate Modelling

Analysis of CMIP6 climate simulations and future climate projections.

Artificial Intelligence Platforms
